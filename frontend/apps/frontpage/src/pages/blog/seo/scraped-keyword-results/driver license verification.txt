The Visual Clues to Spotting a Fake California Driver's License The growing proliferation of fake identification documents requires everyone to stay vigilant in identifying valid credentials from forged ones. Since an estimated 10% of California drivers are unlicensed, it is important to know how to identify a forged California driver's license. The most important element of verifying a California driver’s license is to observe the visual details. Fake driver’s license will often differ from state-issued IDs in terms of font, color, and signature placement. A legitimate ID will also have a ghost image or duplicate portrait in the background. The text on a valid California driver's license is usually positioned in specific locations, with the name located at the center and the driver's license number typically just below it. The expiration date should be easy to read and located near the signature. All valid IDs should also display the name and address of the California Department of Motor Vehicles. The hologram on a valid California driver's license will show either the state seal or the word "California" when rotated in the light.
