What is Reverification?

Reverification is the process of re-verifying the identity of a customer or user who has already verified their identity. This process is triggered by various events, such as changes to key account information, document expiration, high-risk transactions, or suspicious activity. Reverification is an automated process that involves manual review as necessary.

With Footprint's triple binding identity approach, verifying the person behind the screen, their device, and their phone number, reverification becomes a robust and accurate process.


Why is Reverification Important?

Reverification is essential for businesses that are subject to Know Your Customer (KYC) regulations. It's a requirement for continuous monitoring, which includes periodic reverification and ongoing screening against various lists, such as sanctions lists and politically exposed persons lists.

Even if your business isn't subject to KYC regulations, reverification makes sense as it helps prevent fraudulent activity and protects your customers. Footprint's solution offers continuous AML monitoring, ensuring that your business remains compliant with regulations.

Types of Reverification

There are several types of reverification, including:

1. Document Reverification

This involves re-verifying documents used to initially verify a user's identity, such as proof of address, income, or employment. Footprint's enhanced document validation capabilities make this process more efficient.

To learn more about document verification, visit our blog post on document verification.

2. Government ID Reverification

This involves re-verifying government-issued IDs, such as driver's licenses or passports. Footprint's solution uses app clips and passkeys to provide a secure and user-friendly experience.

To learn more about driver's license verification, visit our blog post on driver's license verification.

3. Selfie Reverification

This involves re-verifying a user's identity through selfie verification, which uses liveness detection and compares the selfie to a previously taken selfie or photo ID. Footprint's selfie duplicate detection capabilities prevent identity fraud.

4. Continuous Reports

This involves periodic screening of users against various lists and reports, such as sanctions lists and adverse media reports. Footprint's solution provides real-time risk-based friction adjustment, ensuring that legitimate users are not unnecessarily inconvenienced.


Challenges of Reverification

While reverification is essential, it's not without its challenges. One of the significant challenges is friction, which can frustrate legitimate users who may not understand why they're being asked to reverify their identity.

However, reverification doesn't need to be a heavy lift. Selfie reverification, for instance, can be an efficient and effective means of confirming whether an account has been compromised. Best Practices and Recommendations

To make reverification more effective, businesses should follow these best practices:

* Implement reverification at key touchpoints

Reverify users when they change key account information, documents expire, or when they initiate high-risk transactions.

* Use a combination of reverification methods

Use a combination of document reverification, government ID reverification, selfie reverification, and continuous reports to ensure comprehensive protection.

Use clear and concise language to explain the reverification process to users, and make sure the process is easy to follow.
