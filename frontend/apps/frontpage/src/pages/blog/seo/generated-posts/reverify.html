In today's digital landscape, identity verification is no longer a one-time process. With the rise of identity theft, account takeovers, and financial crimes, it's essential to periodically reverify your users to ensure their identities remain authentic.

In this blog post, we'll explore the concept of reverification, its challenges, and how Footprint's cutting-edge solution can help.


What is Reverification?

Reverification is the process of re-verifying the identity of a customer or user who has already verified their identity. This process is crucial in maintaining the integrity of your user base and preventing fraudulent activities.

Reverification can be triggered by various events, such as:

  • Changes to key account information
  • Document expiration
  • High-risk transactions
  • Suspicious activity
  • Data breaches

Why is Reverification Important?

Reverification is not only a requirement for businesses subject to Know Your Customer (KYC) regulations, but it's also essential in protecting your customers and business from identity theft and financial crimes.

By periodically reverifying your users, you can:

  • Prevent account takeovers and identity theft
  • Detect and prevent financial crimes, such as money laundering
  • Maintain compliance with KYC regulations
  • Build trust with your customers

Challenges of Reverification

While reverification is crucial, it can also introduce friction into the user experience. Reverification processes can be cumbersome, leading to frustration and abandonment.

Additionally, reverification can be a manual process, requiring significant resources and time.
