Electronic Know Your Customer (eKYC) is a digital process of verifying the identity of clients, meeting Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements digitally. It assesses their suitability and the potential risks of illegal intentions in business relationships.

How Does eKYC Work?

The eKYC process typically involves the following steps:

1. Customer Onboarding

The customer submits their identity documents through a secure online platform or mobile application. Footprint's onboarding controls enable businesses to require attestable user experiences, collect additional forms of identification, and perform enhanced device checks to ensure the human behind the computer is who they claim to be.

This process is also known as customer onboarding KYC.

2. Document Verification

The documents are verified using advanced verification measures, such as Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and biometric authentication. Footprint's enhanced document validation capabilities ensure accurate verification of identity documents.

Learn more about document verification.

3. Risk Assessment

The customer's profile is analyzed for potential fraud or money laundering risks using artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms. Footprint's automated suspicious behavioral analysis detects anomalous behavior, such as typing hesitancy and devices on bad reputation networks.

This helps in fraud detection in banking.

4. Compliance Check

The customer's application is checked for compliance with regulatory requirements. Footprint's platform ensures compliance with evolving regulatory frameworks, which is essential for AML compliance.

5. Approval or Rejection

The customer's application is approved or rejected based on the verification and risk assessment results.


Technologies Powering eKYC

Several technologies power eKYC, including:

1. Biometric Authentication

Facial recognition, fingerprint scans, iris scans, and voice recognition are used to verify identities. Footprint's triple binding identity approach verifies the person behind the screen, their device, and their phone number, ensuring robust and accurate identity verification.

2. Optical Character Recognition (OCR)

Extracts relevant information from identity documents.

3. Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML)

Analyze vast datasets, recognize patterns, and continuously learn from new information to improve identity verification accuracy. Footprint's AI-powered platform detects and prevents fraud, raising the cost of fraud for adversaries.

This technology is also used in KYC fraud detection.

4. Blockchain

Provides a secure and decentralized method of storing and verifying customer data. Footprint's seamless integration of onboarding with vaulting enables secure storage of sensitive user data.
