Digital KYC, or Know Your Customer, is an online method of verifying people's identity, enabling them to access any financial instrument in the market. It involves sharing a live photo or video and officially valid documents with the company's representative, which are then verified against official records.

Footprint, a pioneer in digital identity verification, offers a comprehensive platform that streamlines onboarding and ensures accurate identity verification.


Types of Online or Digital KYC Verification

  1. Online KYC form: The customer must fill out an online KYC form and submit it to the respective organization, attaching their signature using either a printout or digital signature. Footprint's onboarding controls enable businesses to require attestable user experiences and collect additional forms of identification.
  2. Video-based KYC: The video-based KYC procedure involves a complete audio-visual interaction wherein the registered officer captures the customer's live photo, video, and valid documents. Footprint's user behavior and device insights detect anomalous behavior, such as typing hesitancy, to prevent identity theft.
  3. OTP-based KYC: OTP-based KYC verification is used to open digital wallets or bank accounts, where the customer enters their primary details and receives an OTP on their mobile number registered with the Aadhaar card. Footprint's triple binding identity approach verifies the person behind the screen, their device, and their phone number, ensuring a robust identity verification process.

Why is Digital KYC Required?

Digital KYC is required to prevent identity thefts, financial frauds, and money laundering. It helps organizations to onboard more customers, improve the customer onboarding experience, and increase revenue.

Footprint's platform addresses these concerns by providing a comprehensive solution that ensures accurate identity verification and secure data storage.
