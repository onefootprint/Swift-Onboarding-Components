What is Electronic Know Your Customer (eKYC)?

Electronic Know Your Customer (eKYC) is a digital process of verifying the identity of customers, which is a mandatory requirement for financial institutions and other organizations to prevent money laundering, terrorism financing, and other financial crimes.

The process involves collecting and verifying customer information, such as name, address, date of birth, and identity documents, through digital means.

eKYC Process

In various countries, eKYC is based on unique identification numbers or documents, such as Aadhaar in India, which is issued by the Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI).

The process involves obtaining explicit consent from the customer, submitting identification details, and verifying the information through the relevant database.


Modes of eKYC

eKYC can be performed through various modes, including:

  1. Aadhaar-based eKYC: This involves verifying the customer's identity through their Aadhaar number.
  2. Video KYC: This involves a live video interaction between the customer and a representative of the financial institution.
  3. Digital KYC: This involves submitting digital documents, such as scanned copies of identity proof and address proof. Learn more about Digital KYC.

Benefits of eKYC

The benefits of eKYC include:

  1. Convenience: eKYC can be performed remotely, eliminating the need for physical presence.
  2. Time-saving: The process is faster and more efficient compared to traditional KYC methods.
  3. Cost-effective: eKYC reduces the costs associated with manual verification and documentation.
  4. Enhanced security: eKYC uses advanced technology, such as biometric authentication and encryption, to ensure the security of customer information.

Challenges of eKYC

However, eKYC also has some challenges, including:

  1. Technical issues: Technical glitches or connectivity problems can hinder the eKYC process.
  2. Security risks: eKYC involves the transmission of sensitive customer information, which can be vulnerable to cyber threats.
  3. Regulatory compliance: Financial institutions must ensure that their eKYC processes comply with regulatory requirements, such as AML compliance.
