What is Synthetic Identity Fraud?

Synthetic identity fraud occurs when a fraudster creates a new identity using a combination of real and fabricated information. This can include a real social security number (SSN) paired with a fake name, date of birth, and address. The goal is to create a new identity that is not associated with a real person, making it difficult to detect and track.

Learn more about synthetic identity theft and how it affects businesses.

Step 1: Gathering Information

Fraudsters gather personal information from various sources, including:

  • Stolen or purchased data from the dark web
  • Public records and social media profiles
  • Phishing scams and other online attacks, often targeting personally identifiable information (PII).

Step 2: Creating the Synthetic Identity

Using the gathered information, the fraudster creates a new identity by combining real and fake data. This can include:

  • Using a real SSN with a fake name and date of birth
  • Creating a new address or using a fake one
  • Using a fake phone number or email address, making it challenging for identity verification tools to detect.

Step 3: Building a Credit Profile

The fraudster applies for credit using the synthetic identity, often starting with small loans or credit cards. By making payments on time, the fraudster builds a positive credit history, making it easier to obtain larger loans and credit lines.

Step 4: Expanding the Synthetic Identity

The fraudster continues to build the synthetic identity by:

  • Opening new accounts and credit lines
  • Applying for loans and mortgages
  • Using the synthetic identity to make purchases and transactions, which can be difficult to detect without KYC fraud detection measures in place.

Step 5: Cashing Out

Once the synthetic identity has a established credit history, the fraudster "cashes out" by maxing out credit lines, taking out large loans, and disappearing with the funds.
