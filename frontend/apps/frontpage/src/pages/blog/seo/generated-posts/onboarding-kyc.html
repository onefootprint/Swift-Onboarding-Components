The KYC onboarding process involves verifying the identity of customers and assessing their risk level before providing them with financial services.

Key Components of KYC Onboarding

1. Customer Identification Program (CIP)

Collecting and verifying customer information, such as name, date of birth, and address.

Footprint's onboarding controls enable businesses to require attestable user experiences, collect additional forms of identification, and perform enhanced device checks to ensure the human behind the computer is who they claim to be.

This process is also known as Customer Identification Program.

2. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

Verifying customer identity and assessing risk level through background checks and verification of documents.

Footprint's user behavior and device insights provide automated suspicious behavioral analysis that detects anomalous behavior, such as typing hesitancy, copy-paste for sensitive fields, devices on bad reputation networks, and more.

Learn more about document verification here.

3. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Conducting additional checks for high-risk customers, including politically exposed persons and those involved in high-risk transactions.

Footprint's additional verifications include enhanced document validation, motor vehicle history, and non-documentary verifications for Mexico and Canada.

4. Ongoing Monitoring

Continuously monitoring customer transactions and updating risk assessments to prevent financial crimes.

Footprint's duplicate and synthetic fraud detection capabilities provide advanced detection of duplicate and synthetic identities, including selfie duplicate detection and identity data de-duplication.
