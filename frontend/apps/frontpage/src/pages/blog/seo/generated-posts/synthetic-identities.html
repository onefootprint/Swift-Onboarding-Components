How Synthetic Identity Theft Works

Synthetic identity theft typically involves stealing a person's Social Security number (SSN) and combining it with fabricated information, such as a name, date of birth, mailing address, email account, and phone number.

This new identity is then used to commit financial fraud, such as applying for loans, bank accounts, credit cards, and more.

Types of Synthetic Identity Theft

There are three main types of synthetic identity theft:

  1. Identity compilation: A fraudster steals a person's SSN and combines it with invented personally identifiable information.
  2. Identity manipulation: A fraudster steals a person's personally identifiable information, alters it slightly, and attempts to pass it off as a new person.
  3. Identity fabrication: A fraudster creates a totally false identity using bogus personally identifiable information.

Protecting Against Synthetic Identity Theft

To prevent synthetic identity theft, individuals can take several steps:

  1. Secure your SSN: Keep your Social Security card at home in a safe place, and avoid repeating your SSN aloud within earshot of others.
  2. Use digital security software: Consider a comprehensive suite of protective digital security software to protect your personal information and passwords.
  3. Know the signs of phishing scams: Be cautious of unexpected communications from government agencies, banks, or financial organizations that lack specific information, such as your name and account number.
  4. Review your credit reports and monitor your credit scores: Regularly check your credit reports and keep tabs on your credit scores to detect any incorrect or unfamiliar information.

By taking these precautions, individuals can help protect themselves against synthetic identity theft and minimize the risk of financial fraud.

Best Practices and Recommendations

To combat synthetic identity theft, businesses and individuals should:

  1. Implement robust identity verification processes: Use a combination of document verification, biometric identification, and database verification to ensure the authenticity of identities.
  2. Monitor credit reports and scores regularly: Regularly review credit reports and scores to detect any suspicious activity or incorrect information.
  3. Use advanced fraud detection tools: Leverage machine learning and artificial intelligence to identify patterns of behavior that may indicate fraudulent activity.
  4. Collaborate with other industry partners and law enforcement: Share information and best practices to stay ahead of evolving threats and prevent synthetic identity theft.
