In today's digital age, onboarding has become a critical process for businesses, especially in regulated industries such as finance, banking, and fintech. Digital onboarding has revolutionized the way companies integrate new customers, employees, or users into their systems, making it faster, more efficient, and convenient.


What is Digital Onboarding?

Digital onboarding is the electronic and remote process of registering new customers, employees, or users. It involves collecting and verifying personal data, identity documents, and other relevant information to ensure compliance with regulatory requirements, such as Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML).

Digital onboarding uses online tools, such as websites, mobile apps, and digital signatures, to simplify and expedite the process. Leading solutions like Footprint's comprehensive platform streamline onboarding, ensuring accurate identity verification and safeguarding sensitive user data.


Types of Digital Onboarding

There are several types of digital onboarding, including:

  1. Manual Process

    Allows KYC officers and customers to complete the onboarding process at their own pace.

  2. Automated Process

    Uses technology to automate the onboarding process, making it faster and more efficient. Footprint's innovative approach leverages native device experiences and strong cryptography to provide a secure and user-friendly onboarding experience.

  3. Assisted Process

    Involves a remote method that keeps customers connected with KYC agents through video calls or other means.

  4. NFC-based Digital Onboarding Process

    Utilizes Near Field Communication (NFC) technology to verify identity documents and other data in real-time.
