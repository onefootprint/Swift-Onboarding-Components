Synthetic identity fraud is a sophisticated type of fraud that involves creating a fake identity by combining real and fake information. This can include using a real Social Security number with a fake name, date of birth, and address.

The goal of synthetic identity fraud is to create a new identity that can be used to open bank accounts, apply for loans, and make purchases, all while avoiding detection by traditional fraud monitoring systems.

What is Synthetic Identity Fraud?

Synthetic identity fraud can be difficult to detect because the fake identity is often designed to look legitimate. Thieves may use stolen Social Security numbers from children, the elderly, or homeless individuals, who may not be actively using their credit or monitoring their credit reports.

This can make it harder to detect the fraud, as there may not be any obvious signs of suspicious activity. You can learn more about how to create a synthetic identity in our step-by-step guide.

Synthetic identity theft is a growing concern, with losses estimated to be in the billions of dollars. It is often used to commit financial fraud, such as applying for loans or credit cards and then defaulting on payments.

It can also be used to hide from authorities or to commit other types of crimes. Learn more about synthetic identity theft.

Protecting Against Synthetic Identity Fraud

To protect against synthetic identity fraud, it is essential to secure your Social Security number and other personally identifiable information.

This can include shredding documents that contain sensitive information, using digital security software, and being wary of phishing scams. Regularly reviewing your credit reports and monitoring your credit scores can also help to detect any suspicious activity.

If you suspect that you are a victim of synthetic identity theft, it is crucial to report the crime to the authorities and to take steps to protect your credit and identity.

This can include placing a fraud alert on your credit reports, freezing your credit, and using a credit monitoring service.
