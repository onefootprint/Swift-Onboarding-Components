What is Document Verification?

Document verification is the process of verifying the authenticity and validity of a document, such as a passport, driver's license, or ID card.

This process is essential for businesses that deal with sensitive information and high-value transactions, as it helps to prevent identity theft, fraud, and other types of cybercrime.

Footprint, a pioneer in identity verification and onboarding, offers a comprehensive platform that streamlines document verification, ensuring accurate and efficient authentication.


Why is Document Verification Important?

Document verification is crucial in today's digital age, where identity theft and fraud are on the rise.

Without proper authentication, businesses can fall victim to fake or stolen documents, leading to financial losses and damage to their reputation.

Document verification helps to ensure that the information provided by customers is accurate and legitimate, reducing the risk of fraud and ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements, such as Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations and Customer Identification Program (CIP).


How Does Document Verification Work?

The document verification process typically involves the following steps:

1. Document Collection

The customer provides a document, such as a passport or driver's license, as part of the account opening process.

2. Data Extraction

The document is scanned or uploaded, and the relevant information is extracted using optical character recognition (OCR) technology.

3. Document Validation

The extracted information is compared to the original document to ensure that it is authentic and has not been tampered with.

Footprint's advanced document validation capabilities, including enhanced document validation and motor vehicle history, provide a robust and accurate verification process, similar to those used in driver's license validation checks.

4. Verification

The document is verified against a database of known documents to ensure that it is legitimate.


Types of Document Verification

There are two main types of document verification: physical and digital.

Physical Document Verification

Physical document verification involves examining the physical attributes of a document, such as its texture or color, to ensure its authenticity.

Digital Document Verification

Digital document verification uses AI-powered software tools to verify the authenticity of documents online, often used in conjunction with Know Your Customer (KYC) tools.
