What is Synthetic Identity Theft?

Synthetic identity theft is a type of identity theft where a thief creates a new identity by combining real and fake information. This can include using a real Social Security number with a fake name, address, and date of birth. The thief may also use fake identification documents, such as a driver's license or passport, to support the new identity.


How Does Synthetic Identity Theft Work?

Synthetic identity theft typically involves the following steps:

  1. Gathering personal information: The thief collects personal information, such as Social Security numbers, dates of birth, and addresses, from various sources, including data breaches, social media, and public records.
  2. Creating a synthetic identity: The thief combines the real and fake information to create a new identity.
  3. Building a credit profile: The thief uses the synthetic identity to apply for credit and build a credit profile.
  4. Committing fraud: The thief uses the synthetic identity to commit fraud, such as applying for loans or credit cards, and then defaulting on payments.

Why is Synthetic Identity Theft a Problem?

Synthetic identity theft is a problem because it can be difficult to detect and can result in significant financial losses for individuals and businesses. The thief may use the synthetic identity to commit multiple types of fraud, including credit card fraud, loan fraud, and tax refund fraud.
